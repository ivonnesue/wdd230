Welcome to the Marysville Chamber of Commerce

Marysville, The Oldest Little City in California

Marysville, California

Marysville is a city and the county seat of Yuba County, California, located in the Gold Country region of Northern California. As of the 2010 United States Census, the population was 12,072, reflecting a decrease of 196 from the 12,268 counted in the 2000 Census. It is part of the Yuba-Sutter area of Greater Sacramento. Here are some interesting places and fun things to do in Marysville

Bok Kai Temple

The Bok Kai Temple is a traditional Chinese temple, the oldest fully operated temple in Northern California. It was built in 1880 during the Gold Rush in California. The temple served as a center for for Chinese immigrant and today it is a place of worship and a California historical landmark. There is also a Museum where historical chinese artifacts are exhibited. Visit the Temple

Ellis Lake

Ellis Lake is a man-made lake. Ellis Lake was once a spillway of the Feather River. It wasn't until 1924 that the Women's Improvement Club of Marysville commissioned John McClaren, designer of the Golden Gate Park in San Francisco, to turn the swamp into a lake. The project was completed in 1939. The lake was named for Marysville citizen W.T. Ellis, Jr. Visit Ellis Lake

West Coast Falconry

If you are interested in nature and animal conservation, you may want to try a falconry experience. West Coast Falconry is your premiere Northern California resource for a full range of hands-on raptor and falconry classes and services. Learn more.

Mary Aaron Museum

Originally a home, built in the 1850s by invertor and archited Warren P. Miller and intended to be his residence. This Gothic Revival home was purchased by the Aaron family in the 1870s. It remained in the family until 1955. At that time, the only son of Mary Bobo Aaron gifted it to the City of Marysville to be maintained as a museum in honor of his mother. Mary Bobo Aaron. The museum houses a collection archives, photos and items related to history of the City.

The Peach Festival

Enjoy dozens of delectable peach foods, desserts and beverages while visiting one of more than 150 vendors spaces. Dance to the distinctive sounds of area bands serenading festival-goers at one of our two entertainment stages. The Marysville Peach Festival held the third weekend of July each year.
